# SLA vs SLO vs SLI

**SLA (Service Level Agreement):**
SLA is a formal agreement between the service provider and customer that defines the expected service level and business consequences if the target is not achieved.

**SLO (Service Level Objective):**
SLO is the internal reliability target that the team aims to achieve for a service.

**SLI (Service Level Indicator):**
SLI is the actual measurement used to evaluate service performance.

Examples:
- Availability: 99.99%
- Response time: less than 200 ms
- Error rate: less than 1%
