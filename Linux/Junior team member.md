
How do you mentor junior Linux engineers?

When a junior Linux administrator joins the team, I support their onboarding through structured knowledge transfer sessions where I explain the environment, architecture, and standard operational procedures.

I walk them through our runbooks, common troubleshooting scenarios, and incident RCA discussions so they understand both the system and real production issues.

During daily operational calls, I actively explain ongoing issues in simple terms so they can understand the impact, root cause, and resolution steps.

I also follow a buddy system where they shadow me during troubleshooting, and gradually I let them handle low-risk tickets under supervision.

This approach helps them become independent faster while ensuring production stability is not impacted.
